# MathHoot — Features

*Pre-existing, new, and upcoming*

Live at **https://wmeleis.github.io/mathhoot/**.

## Pre-existing features

- **Procedurally generated questions** across ten topics — Arithmetic, Fractions & Percents, Algebra, Powers & Roots, Geometry, Number Theory, Statistics, Sequences, Trigonometry, and Calculus — so no two games are alike.
- **Three question types:** numerical, conceptual, and true/false.
- **Five difficulty levels** (Easy → College) plus **Adaptive**, which auto-adjusts as you answer.
- **Configurable game:** 5–20 questions, an optional per-question timer, and time-based (Kahoot-style) or correct-count scoring.
- **Kahoot-style play:** one question at a time on color-coded answer tiles with a live countdown.
- **Results that teach:** a report card (% correct per subject) and a question-by-question review with short explanations and direct Khan Academy links.
- **Custom question sets:** build your own by typing questions or picking from MathHoot's bank, then share via link or short code.
- **Free accounts (teacher / student)** that sync sets, history, and preferences across devices.
- **Personalized recommendations** for students — weak-topic detection and a targeted, auto-built practice set.
- **Live classroom games:** a teacher hosts, students join with a code, and everyone plays in real time with a live leaderboard.

## New features

- **AI Integration** — describe a quiz in plain English and Claude (via the Claude API) writes the whole set: questions, four answer choices, the correct answer, and an explanation each, saved to your sets. Uses your own Anthropic API key, kept only in your browser.
- **Accessibility & interface language** *(newest)* — high-contrast mode, larger text, readable spacing, reduced motion, and screen-reader labels on the question and answer tiles; plus an interface-language selector (English / Español). Settings are saved per device and to your account. (Topic names and question content remain in their original form for now.)
- **Clickable question catalog** — the catalog shows how many templates exist per topic and difficulty level; tap any number to view the templates themselves.
- **In-game difficulty label** — every question now shows the difficulty level it was generated at.
- **Quit button** — leave a quiz mid-game and return to setup.
- **Password reset** — reset a forgotten account password by email.

## Upcoming features

- **More question formats** — free numeric entry (type the answer), multi-step problems, drag-to-order, and image/diagram questions (geometry figures, graphs).
- **Class analytics dashboard** — per-student and per-class mastery by topic and trends over time, beyond a single game's leaderboard.
- **Assignments / homework mode** — assign a set with a due date; students complete it on their own time and results roll up to the teacher.
- **Printable worksheets** — export any set as a PDF worksheet with an answer key, for offline or homework use.

*This is an early working draft; the upcoming features are planned, not yet built.*
